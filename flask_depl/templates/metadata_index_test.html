BREAST CANCER

Overview:

Cancer is simply defined as abnormal growth of cells. Our whole body is made up of cells that act and grow in controlled ways as per the needs.
They are genetically controlled. If any of the genes in these cells is damaged, then the cell can grow out of control thus eventually leading to cancer.
Cancer cells quickly grow and divide. This happens even when there's no enough space for growth and also nutrients shortage .
They can spread to other parts of the body. Tumors, masses, or lesions are names for abnormal growths of cells that can become cancer.


Breast cancer is cancer that develops from breast tissue. The term "cancer" is used when a tumor is malignant,
which is to say it has the potential to cause harm, including death. Tumors can be benign (noncancerous) or malignant (cancerous). Benign tumors tend to grow slowly
and do not spread. Malignant tumors can grow rapidly, invade and destroy nearby normal tissues, and spread throughout the body.


Symptoms:
  • Fatigue.
  • Lump or area of thickening that can be felt under the skin.
  • Weight changes, including unintended loss or gain.
  • Skin changes, such as yellowing, darkening or redness of the skin, sores that won't heal, or changes to existing moles.
  • Changes in bowel or bladder habits.
  • Persistent cough or trouble breathing.

Causes:
  • Regualar smoking
  • Radiation Exposure
  • Usage of cancer-causing chemicals (carcinogens)
  • hormones
  • Family History and Genetics

Effects:
  • Can spread to other parts of the body
  • Bleeding and bruising (thrombocytopenia).
  • Bone Density loss (osteoporosis).
  • Severe Abdominal Bloating
  • Severe Fatigue.
  • Edema
  • Seizures

Parameters considered for training:
  • Radius_mean, Texture_mean
  • Perimeter_mean, Area-mean
  • Smoothness_mean, Compactness_mean
  • Pcc, Concave points_mean
  • Symmetry_mean, Radius_se
  • Perimeter_se, Area_se
  • Compactness_se, Concavity_se
  • Concave points_se, Fractal_dimension_se
  • Radius_worst, Texture_worst
  • Perimeter_worst, Area_worst
  • Smoothness_worst, Compactness_worst
  • Concavity_worst, Symmetry_worst
  • Fractal_dimension_worst, Concavity_mean
  • Fractal_dimension_mean
  • texture_se, smoothness_se

From our research these parameters had a significant role in prediction.


Precautions & Preventions:
  • Avoid using tobacco. Using any type of tobacco puts you on a collision course with cancer.
  • Eat healthy.
  • Maintain a healthy weight and be physically active.
  • Examine your breasts every month after age 20.
  • Get regular medical care and follow hygienic habits.


Arrhythmia

Overview:

The term "arrhythmia" refers to any change from the normal sequence of electrical impulses of heart. The impulses be happen too fast or too slow.
causing the heart to beat abnormally. When the heart doesn't beat properly, it can't pump blood effectively..thus
the lungs, brain and other organs can't work properly and may shut down or be damaged.
The fluctuations can be classified into five categories.


Atrial fibrillation is an irregular and often very rapid heart rhythm (arrhythmia) that can lead to blood clots in the heart.
Bradycardia is a slow heart rate. An adults at rest usually has a beat between 60 and 100 times a minute.
If one has bradycardia, the heart beats fewer than 60 times a minute.
A conduction disorder, also known as heart block, is a problem with the electrical system that controls one's heart rate and rhythm.
Premature ventricular contractionsare extra heartbeats that begin in one of the heart's two lower pumping chambers (ventricles).
These extra beats disrupt the regular heart rhythm, sometimes causing a sensation of a fluttering or a skipped beat in the chest.
Ventricular fibrillation is a type of abnormal heart rhythm where disorganized heart signals cause the lower heart chambers (ventricles) to twitch uselessly.
As a result, the heart doesn't pump blood to the rest of the body.


Symptoms:
  • A fluttering feeling in the chest
  • A racing heartbeat (tachycardia)
  • A slow heartbeat (bradycardia)
  • Chest pain
  • Shortness of breath
  • Anxiety
  • Fatigue
  • Light-headedness or dizziness
  • Fainting (syncope) or near fainting

Causes:
  • Current heart attack or scarring from a previous one.
  • Blocked arteries in the heart (coronary artery disease)
  • Changes to the heart's structure, such as from cardiomyopathy.
  • Diabetes.
  • High blood pressure.
  • Infection with COVID-19.
  • Overactive thyroid gland .(hyperthyroidism)
  • Underactive thyroid gland. (hypothyroidism)
  • Certain medications, including cold and allergy drugs bought without a prescription.
  • Drinking too much alcohol or caffeine.
  • Drug abuse.
  • Genetics.
  • Smoking.
  • Stress or anxiety.

Effects:

If not treated, arrhythmia can:

  • Damage the heart, brain, or other organs
  • Cause stroke, Cardiac arrest or heart failures.
  • Cause Severe Fatigue
  • Cause Seizures
  • Cause breathlessness

Parameters considered for Training:
  • Atrial Fibrillation
  • Bradycardia
  • Conduction Disorder
  • Premature contraction
  • Tachycardia
  • Ventricular Fibrillation
  • Normal

We trained and tested our model on 15,341, 6,825 quality ECG Grey scale images respectively and these had prominent role in prediction.

Precautions & Preventions:
  • Eating a heart-healthy diet.
  • Staying physically active.
  • Reducing smoking.
  • Limiting or avoiding caffeine and alcohol.
  • Reducing stress, as intense stress and anger can cause arrhythmia.
  • Using medications as directed and informing doctor about all the medicines you take, including those bought without a prescription.


Liver Disease

Overview:

liver disease applies to many conditions that stop the liver from working or prevent it from functioning well.
The liver is body's largest internal organ and performs hundreds of tasks associated with food digestion, energy storage and waste filtering.
Thus, any damage made to liver builds up over time if left untreated.
Liver disease is also called hepatic disease


Most common types of liver disease include, 'Hepatitis'- defined as an inflammation of liver,
Fatty Liver Disease- defined as a condition in which too much fat builds up in your liver,
Cirrhosis- defined as the last stage of scarring (fibrosis) of liver, liver cancer.


Symptoms:
  • Skin and eyes that appear yellowish (jaundice)
  • Abdominal pain and swelling
  • Swelling in the legs and ankles
  • Itchy skin
  • Dark urine color
  • Pale stool color
  • Chronic fatigue
  • Nausea or vomiting

Causes:
  • Heavy alcohol use
  • Obesity
  • Infection with COVID-19
  • Certain medications, including cold and allergy drugs bought without a prescription
  • Drug abuse
  • Genetics
  • Exposure to other people's blood and body fluids

Effects:

If not treated, Liver disease can:

  • Damage liver
  • Cause Liver Failure
  • Cause slow kidney failure
  • Cause Nail Discoloration
  • Cause Night Blindness

Parameters considered for training:
  • Total Bilirubin, Direct Bilirubin
  • Alamine Aminotransferase, Aspartate Aminotransferase
  • Alkalin Phosphotase, Total Proteins
  • Albumin, Albumin and Globulin Ratio

From our research these parameters had a significant role in prediction.


Precautions & Preventions:
  • Drink alcohol in moderation
  • Get vaccinated
  • Use medications wisely
  • Avoid contact with other people's blood and body fluids
  • Take care with aerosol sprays
  • Be hygienic


Malaria

Overview:

Malaria is a protozoan infection of the red blood cells, transmitted by the bite of a female anopheles mosquito.
While widespread throughout the tropics, malaria has been virtually eliminated by waste filtering from temperate climates.
The severity of malaria varies based on the species of plasmodium.

When a mosquito bites someone who has malaria, the mosquito becomes infected.
When that mosquito bites someone else, it transfers a parasite to the other person’s bloodstream. There, the parasites multiply.
There are five types of malaria parasites that can infect humans.
In rare cases, people who are pregnant and who have malaria can transfer the disease to their children before or during birth.
It’s possible, but unlikely, for malaria to be passed through blood transfusions, organ donations and hypodermic needles.


Symptoms:
  • Fever
  • Chills
  • General feeling of discomfort
  • Headache
  • Nausea and vomiting
  • Diarrhea
  • Abdominal pain
  • Muscle or joint pain
  • Fatigue
  • Rapid breathing
  • Rapid heart rate
  • Cough

Causes:
  • organ transplant
  • use of shared needles or syringes
  • A transfusion
  • Unhygenic environments promote malarial spread

Effects:

Malaria can cause a number of life-threatening complications. The following may occur:

  • Low blood sugar
  • Swelling of the blood vessels of the brain, or cerebral malaria
  • An accumulation of fluid in the lungs that causes breathing problems, or pulmonary edema
  • Organ failure of the kidneys, liver, or spleen
  • Anemia due to the destruction of red blood cells

Parameters considered for training:

  • Parasitized
  • Uninfected

We trained and tested our model on 20,670, 6,890 quality Malarial cell images respectively and these had prominent role in prediction.


Precautions & Preventions:
  • Apply mosquito repellent with DEET (diethyltoluamide) to exposed skin
  • Drape mosquito netting over beds
  • Put screens on windows and doors
  • Treat clothing, mosquito nets, tents, sleeping bags
  • Wear long pants and long sleeves to cover your skin
  • Maintain hygienic environments


Kidney Disease

Overview:

It is a condition where your kidneys are damaged and can't filter blood the way they should.
You are at greater risk for kidney disease if you have diabetes or high blood pressure.
If you experience kidney failure, treatments include kidney transplant or dialysis.


Common types of kidneey disease include, Chronic kidney disease - a condition where kidney function will deteriorate
to the point where they can no longer properly perform their job.
Kidney stones - Minerals and other substances in your blood may crystallize in the kidneys, forming solid particles,
or stones, that usually pass out of your body in urine.
Polycystic kidney disease - It is a genetic disorder that causes many cysts, round sacs of fluid, to grow inside
and on the surfaces of your kidneys, interfering with kidney function.
Urinary tract infections - They are bacterial infections of any of the parts of your urinary system.
Infections in the bladder and urethra are most common. They’re generally easily treatable and have few, if any, long-term consequences.
However, if left untreated, these infections can spread to the kidneys and lead to kidney failure.

Symptoms:
  • Weight loss and poor appetite
  • Swollen ankles, feet or hands – as a result of water retention (edema)
  • Shortness of breath
  • Tiredness
  • Blood in urine
  • An increased need to urination particularly at night
  • Sleeping Disorders (insomnia)
  • Itchy skin

Causes:
  • Diabetes
  • High blood pressure
  • Heart (cardiovascular) diseases
  • Smoking
  • Obesity
  • Genetic
  • Abnormal kidney structure

Effects:
  • Nausea
  • Vomiting
  • Loss of appetite
  • Fatigue and weakness
  • Variations in Urination
  • Decreased mental sharpness
  • Increased Muscle cramps

Parameters considered for training:

  • Specific Gravivity (sg), Albumin (al)
  • Hemoglobin (hemo), Pus cell (pc)
  • Red blood cell count (rc), Hypertension(htn)
  • Appetite (appet), Diabetes Mellitus(dm)

From our research these parameters had a significant role in prediction.


Precautions & Preventions:
  • Manage your blood sugar
  • Manage your blood pressure
  • Maintain a healthy weight
  • Eat a healthy diet
  • Reduce salt intake
  • Drink plenty of water
  • Limit alcohol
  • Limit over-the-counter pain medication
  • Reduce stress


pneumonia Disease

Overview:

Pneumonia is an infection that inflames the air sacs in one or both lungs.
The air sacs may be filled with fluid or pus (purulent material), causing cough with phlegm or pus, fever, chills, and difficulty
breathing. A variety of organisms, including bacteria, viruses and fungi, can cause pneumonia.


Pneumonia can be classified into four types - Bacterial pneumonia, Viral pneumonia, Mycoplasma pneumonia, Fungal pneumonia.
Bacterial Pneumonia is caused by bacteria and might just affect one small area of your lung, or it may encompass all of both of your lungs.
Viral Pneumonia is responsible for about one-third of all pneumonia cases, and is caused by various viruses typically goes away more quickly.
Mycoplasma pneumonia is also known as walking pneumonia causes milder illness and is prominent in young adults.
Fungal pneumonia generally affects people with weak immune systems, including those with AIDS.


Symptoms:
  • Chest pain when you breathe or cough.
  • Confusion or changes in mental awareness (in adults age 65 and older)
  • Cough, which may produce phlegm.
  • Fatigue.
  • Fever, sweating and shaking chills.
  • Lower than normal body temperature (in adults older than age 65 and people with weak immune systems)
  • Nausea, vomiting or diarrhea.

Causes:
  • Virus
  • Bacteria
  • Fungi
  • Flu

Effects:
  • Reduced lung Capacity
  • Weaker Muscles
  • Weight Loss
  • Increased anxiety and stress levels

Parameters considered for training:
  • Pneumonia
  • Normal

We trained and tested our model on 5,232, 624 quality chest x-ray images respectively and these had prominent role in prediction.


Precautions & Preventions:
  • Get a flu shot. Flu is a common cause of pneumonia, so one of the best defenses against contracting pneumonia is to get a flu shot each year.
  • If you are prone to high risk, get vaccinated.
  • Wash your hands regularly.
  • Maintain healthy habits.
  • Limit exposure to throat irritants, including pollution and alcohol


Diabetes

Overview:

It is also called as Diabetes Mellitus and refers to a group of diseases that affect how the body uses blood sugar (glucose).
Glucose is an important source of energy for the cells that make up the muscles and tissues.
It's also the brain's main source of fuel.
no matter what type of diabetes you have, it can lead to excess sugar in the blood.
Too much sugar in the blood can lead to serious health problems.


Diabetes comes in different forms, depending on the cause.
Prediabetes- It is when your blood sugar is higher than it should be but not high enough for your doctor to diagnose diabetes.
Type 1 diabetes- It is also called insulin-dependent diabetes and is an autoimmune condition. It happens when your body attacks your pancreas with antibodies.
The organ is damaged and doesn't make insulin.
Type 2 Diabetes- Here, your pancreas usually creates some insulin. But either it’s not enough or your body doesn’t use it like it should.
Type 2 diabetes is often milder than type 1
Gestational Diabetes- Pregnancy usually causes some form of insulin resistance. If this becomes diabetes, it’s called gestational.
Gestational diabetes is more of a risk for the baby than the mother.
A baby might have unusual weight gain before birth, trouble breathing at birth, or a higher risk of obesity and diabetes later in life.


Symptoms:
  • Feeling more thirsty than usual
  • Urinating often
  • Losing weight without trying
  • Tiredness
  • Presence of ketones in the urine
  • Feeling irritable or having other mood changes
  • Having slow-healing sores
  • Getting a lot of infections, such as gum, skin and vaginal infections
  • Having blurry vision

Causes:
  • Overweight
  • obesity
  • physical inactivity
  • Insulin Resistance
  • Genetic
  • Medicines
  • Hormonal Diseases

Effects:
  • Cataracts
  • Retinopathy
  • Damage to large and small blood vessels
  • Glaucoma
  • Anxiety and Depression
  • Thyroid Fluctuations
  • Nephropathy

Parameters considered for training:

  • No of Pregnancies (preg), Glucose(glu)
  • Blood Pressure (bp), Body Mass Index(bmi)
  • Insulin (insu), Age(age)
  • Diabetes Pedigree Function(dfun), Skin Thickness(skin)

From our research these parameters had a significant role in prediction.


Precautions & Preventions:
  • Manage your blood glucose levels
  • Be more physically active
  • Eat healthy plant foods
  • Eat healthy fats
  • Skip fad diets and make healthier choices
  • Limit alcohol and smoking
